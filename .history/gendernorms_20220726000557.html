What ARE gender norms? And why do they exist?

Gender norms are stereotypes surrounding what a man or women typically is supposed to, or is entitled to do. As defined by the free Dictionary, gender norms are "the overtexpression of attitudes that indicate to others the degree of your maleness or femaleness " in today's spciety based on tradition. Simply, the fact that pink is for girls and blue is for boys are gender norms, and that women are expected to stay in the house and clean while men go out and work for the family's wealth. ALthough those may obviosuly look to be very traditionl worldviews that or world has clearly moved on from, we see gender norms in modern day society: when women get catcalled on the street, or the gender wage gap that many companies face legal issues due to. Though it may seem that these behaviours aren't directly derived from the applications of gender norms, a closer look can show to just how much these traditional ideas, regardless of how wrong and outdated they may seem, still exist in today's world. These normse have existed in ancient civilizations, dating all the way back to Greek and Roman civilizations, when woman would stay at home and tend to the house and children and men would be allowed to leave the house to go to their jobs, enjoy activities for leisure, and to voice political concerns. These norms have been passed down from one generation to the next, through cultures and religions, until it naturally came to every generation that there are certain roles for a woman and a man to fulfill, and these rules cannot be bent.
